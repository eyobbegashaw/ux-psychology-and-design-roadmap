 This file is about cementing the behaviors selected in the previous files by moving them from conscious action (System 2) to automatic routine (System 1). The core loop is Cue -> Routine -> Reward, and our goal is to automate the routine.

**1. The Cue (The Trigger in Context)**
To automate a routine, the cue must be crystal clear, consistent, and contextual. It's a sensor fusion problem.
- *Five Types of Cues:* Time, Location, Preceding Event, Emotional State, Other People.
- *Contextual Dev Example:* A to-do app reminder isn't just "Remind at 5 PM" (Time). A great one uses location: "Remind me to buy milk when I leave my office." This requires a geofence trigger. The most powerful cue is a *preceding event*. "When I close my fitness app, show me a water-tracking prompt." This event chaining is a form of behavioral programming, where the UI state machine listens for one action's completion to fire the cue for the next.

**2. The Routine (The Automated Action)**
This is where we reduce the Behavior Grid's Green Dot to a highly-able, semi-automatic action.
- *Reducing Friction to Zero:* The ultimate form of automation is removing the routine entirely. Auto-save is not a feature; it's the automation of the security routine. Google's Smart Compose is automating the writing routine. As a developer, you are constantly looking for deterministic sequences of user actions that can be collapsed into a single, automated system action.
- *The Siri/Ok Google Paradigm:* Voice interfaces are the ultimate friction-removal for complex routines. "Remind me to call John when I get home" is a single action that programs a time-, location-, and context-based trigger for a future routine. This requires complex natural language processing pipelines and a reliable event-scheduling architecture.

**3. The Reward (The System 1 Signal of Success)**
For an automated routine to be reinforcing, the reward must be immediate and unambiguous.
- *The Closing Ritual:* A "completion state" is a reward. When I complete a daily to-do list, I don't just want the tasks to disappear. I want a "Day Complete" screen with a subtle, triumphant animation. This is a reward of the self, signaling that the automated routine has successfully run its course.
- *Dev as a Habit Architect:* Our goal is to build an architecture that senses (cues), simplifies (routines), and singles (rewards). A user's entire journey can be modeled as a series of nested habit loops. Opening the app (routine) cued by boredom (internal trigger), rewarded with interesting content. Browsing (routine) cued by a headline, rewarded with knowledge. Subscribing (routine) cued by a prompt, rewarded with a feeling of mastery and future access. Each loop is a state machine that we must design to be as robust and frictionless as a well-tuned mechanical watch, creating an ever-tightening behavioral flywheel.
